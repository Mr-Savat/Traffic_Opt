  PROJECT PROPOSAL
  Quantum-Enhanced Traffic Signal Optimization for
  Motorcycle-Dominant Traffic in Phnom Penh
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Year 3 Undergraduate Thesis  |  IEEE-Quality Target
================================================================================


1. PROBLEM
───────────
  Phnom Penh traffic lights use fixed timers that ignore actual
  vehicle queues. With over 70% of traffic being motorcycles,
  this causes significant and unnecessary congestion. No existing
  research applies quantum optimization to this context.

  We choose Quantum Annealing over Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL)
  because QUBO is a combinatorial optimization problem—exactly what
  QA is designed for. DRL requires large training data and is a
  black-box approach, making it less interpretable for traffic decisions.


2. OUR SOLUTION
────────────────
  We formulate traffic signal control as a QUBO (Quadratic
  Unconstrained Binary Optimization) problem with motorcycle-
  dominant weights and solve it using both classical and quantum
  methods. Tested in SUMO traffic simulator with synthetic
  Phnom Penh traffic data.

  "Quantum-Enhanced" refers to the QUBO formulation design
  which is hardware-agnostic and ready for future QPU
  deployment as quantum hardware scales.

  Vehicle weights derived from Phnom Penh modal share (JASIC, 2023):
    Motorcycle  0.60  (modal share: 58–70%)
    Car         0.30  (modal share: 14–20%)
    Tuk-tuk     0.10  (modal share: 10–15%)


3. WHAT WE COMPARE
───────────────────
  Method 1 — Fixed Cycle       Traditional fixed timer (baseline)
  Method 2 — Webster Formula   Classical traffic engineering
  Method 3 — SA + QUBO         Our method, classical solver
  Method 4 — QA + QUBO         Our method, quantum solver (D-Wave)

  Tested under 4 scenarios:
    Rush AM (700 moto/h)  |  Rush PM (600/h)
    Off-Peak (200/h)      |  Extreme Load (900/h)

        motorcycle-dominant scenario


6. SCOPE
─────────
  Single 4-way intersection (proof-of-concept)
  Synthetic SUMO data calibrated to Phnom Penh statistics
  All tools free: Python · SUMO · dimod · neal · D-Wave Leap


7. LIMITATIONS
───────────────
  • Synthetic data only — real sensor validation is future work
  • Single intersection — network extension is future work
  • QA near-optimal only — QUBO design is hardware-agnostic
  • Simplified motorcycle behavior — 85% compliance assumed
